Quantum Free Electron Theory of Metal

Metal

Metals in general have high electrical conductivity, thermal conductivity, luster and density, and the ability to be deformed under stress without cleaving. While there are several metals that have low density, hardness, and melting points, these are extremely reactive and are rarely encountered in their elemental metallic form. Let’s us see some of the characteristic features of metals.

Classical Theory

We can readily explain several of the important physical properties of metals in terms of free electron gas. The motion of the free electrons is responsible for the high electric conductivity of metals. Accordingly, the free electrons are often called conduction electrons. One of the first calculations of the conductivity of a free-electron gas was made by Paul Drude in 1900.

Failure of Classical Theory
